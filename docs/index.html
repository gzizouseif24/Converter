Frequently Asked Questions

What is FLAC and why convert it?

FLAC (Free Lossless Audio Codec) is a high-quality audio format that preserves all audio data. However, FLAC isn't natively supported by iTunes, iOS devices, and many media players. Converting to ALAC maintains the lossless quality while making it compatible with Apple's ecosystem, while MP3 provides universal compatibility at the cost of some audio quality.

Will I lose audio quality during conversion?

When converting to ALAC, the audio quality remains lossless - meaning no quality is lost. When converting to MP3, there will be some quality loss as MP3 is a lossy format, but you can select high bitrates to minimize this loss.

Do I need an internet connection to use the app?

No. Once downloaded and installed, FLAC Converter works completely offline. All processing happens locally on your computer.

Can I convert multiple files simultaneously?

Yes! The app supports batch processing, allowing you to convert multiple files at once. Just select all the files you want to convert and the app will process them sequentially.

Is this application free to use?

Yes, FLAC Converter is completely free and open source. You can use it for personal or commercial purposes without any limitations.

Does it preserve metadata and album art?

Yes, all metadata including artist information, album details, track numbers, and embedded artwork are preserved during the conversion process.
